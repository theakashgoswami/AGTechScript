üìù Filing a DMCA Notice

If you believe that material available on our website infringes your copyright, you may submit a written notification to our Designated Agent. To be effective, your notice must include:

‚úÖ Required Information:

  1. Physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or authorized representative.
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed (or a list of multiple works).
  3. Identification of the infringing material - URLs or specific location on our site.
  4. Your contact information - name, address, phone number, and email.
  5. A statement that you have a good faith belief the use is not authorized.
  6. A statement that the information is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, you are authorized to act.

‚Ü©Ô∏è Counter-Notice Procedure

If you believe your material was removed by mistake or misidentification, you may file a counter-notice. This must include:

  • Your physical or electronic signature
  • Identification of the removed material and its original location
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the material was removed by mistake
  • Your name, address, and phone number
  • A statement consenting to jurisdiction of federal court in your district

Send counter-notice to our Designated Agent with subject: "DMCA COUNTER NOTICE"

If we receive a valid counter-notice, we may restore the removed material in 10-14 business days unless the complaining party files a court action.
